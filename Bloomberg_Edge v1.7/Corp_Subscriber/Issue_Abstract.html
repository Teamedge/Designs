Financial/Business Acumen: “Investing: The Wisdom of Standing Pat ” p. 66

Hydrowatt specializes in generating electricity from turbines in aqueducts and was encouraged by generous renewable energy incentives. A Europe-wide push to get 20% of energy from renewable sources by 2020 has led governments to finance projects through incentives and so-called feed-in tariffs, which require utilities to pay above-market rates for green power.

 

In many nations, economies that can’t generate enough jobs to absorb their young people have created an entire generation of the disaffected, unemployed, or underemployed (including a growing number of recent college graduates) for whom the post-crash economy has little to offer. Instability will result if these young people don’t find a place and society doesn’t harness their energy, intelligence, and enthusiasm. In addition, the world is aging, and in many countries the young are being crushed by a gerontocracy of older workers who stay in the better jobs as long as possible and then retire and demand pensions that the younger generation will be forced to shoulder.


Key Learning Points:

  • High unemployment can stifle job creation and fuel inflation.
  • When there is instability, commodity prices tend to rise.
  • Hoarding and not investing are common responses to instability.
